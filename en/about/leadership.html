Adrian Gil Castillejo
Co-founder & Chief Technology Officer

Eduardo is a Biomedical Engineer graduated from Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey. He has been interested in open source solutions and coding since an early age. Reason that took him into the Entrepreneur Ecosystem. He started as an independent developer for iOS applications and Web newsletters and progress through hardware programming, developing solutions for the Health system. Founded Radiatta productions at age 17 for music and media production, on 2013 he won Startup Weekend 2013 in Guadalajara, Mexico with TeRento.mx a 2nd hand book market for students also the same year he Co-Founded GreenBox a company focused on creating high-end Acoustic Amplifiers for mobile devices.

Currently he volunteers with Mexican RedCross on the implementation of a Biomedical Engineering Department and developing low cost blood pressure Sphygmomanometers.


Biomedical Engineer specializing in electronics and in embedded software applications specifically in data processing and machine learning. Recognized worldwide in these engineering areas obtaining so far two refereed IEEE publications, one SciELO journal publication and one Springer-Verlag monograph chapter, all of them as first author. One of his many recognitions is The Best Paper Award in the area of Human Machine Interaction at the 2013 IEEE International Conference on Human System Interaction due to his research in Brain-Machine Interfaces.

Eduardo graduated in Biomedical Engineering from Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey. He got the highest GPA of his bachelor's degree, obtaining the Honorable Mention of Excellence. He has worked in research laboratories in Germany, Spain, and Mexico, developing technological solutions specifically in the fields of electronics and informatics.


Mechatronics Engineer specialized in 3D design, software development and passionate for human aid device design & manufacture.

He achieved academic excellence diploma and graduated with honours from his Informatics and Computing Major at Centro de Enseñanza Técnica Industrial prior to enrolling in his second major as a Mechatronics Engineer at Instituto Tecnológico de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey. Following his passion Adrian designed a rowing-pedaling bicycle for upper half body rehabilitation, won an Intel contest: “Design and manufacture of an optical text reader with aural and Braille display” and obtained a design and development of Videogames Diploma. In 2013 he won a scholarship from a Canadian organization to go and work at the NeuroLab (NeuroScience Research Lab) at the University of Calgary designing and developing a videogame aimed to allow doctors and care givers to correctly identify children at high risk for Developmental Topographical Disorientation (DTD).
